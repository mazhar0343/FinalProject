Circulatory System

KEEP A HEALTHY HEART!

Veins carry blood from the tissues of the body back to the heart, are usually positioned closer beneath the surface of the skin, are less muscular than arteries, but contain valves to help keep blood flowing in the right direction, usually toward the heart and would collapse if blood flow stops. Arteries carry blood away from the heart to the tissues of the body, are usually positioned deeper within the body, are more muscular than veins, which helps in transporting blood that is full of oxygen efficiently to the tissues, would generally remain open if blood flow stopped, due to their thick muscular layer.
